Where to pray

Prayer spaces near campus

You’re never far from a place to pray around the University of Edinburgh.

🕌

Edinburgh Central Mosque

On Potterrow, right beside central campus — the main mosque for daily prayers and Jumu’ah, just steps from George Square.

🏛️

University Prayer Rooms

Multi-faith and prayer rooms at the Chaplaincy Centre (Bristo Square) and near the Main Library area for quick prayers between lectures.

🧪

King’s Buildings

Science and engineering students have prayer facilities at the King’s Buildings campus — ask at the hub for the current room.

📿

Jumu’ah Prayer

Friday congregational prayer is held at Edinburgh Central Mosque and at university venues — check our Instagram for the weekly khutbah times.

Facing the Ka’bah

The Qibla from Edinburgh

From Edinburgh, the Qibla is approximately 119° — roughly Southeast (ESE). A Qibla compass or a trusted prayer app (such as Muslim Pro or IslamicFinder) will point you precisely.

Most prayer rooms on campus and at the mosque are already marked with the Qibla, so look for the sign or the prayer mat orientation when you arrive.

Halal food near campus

Edinburgh is packed with halal options — here’s where ISoc members tend to gather. (Always confirm halal status directly with each venue.)

🍛

Nicolson Street & Square

A short walk from George Square: kebabs, curries, grills and late-night bites loved by students.

🍜

Newington & Southside

Cosy cafes and restaurants down the South Clerk Street stretch — perfect for a relaxed group meal.

☕

Around Potterrow

Grab a quick halal lunch near the mosque and central campus before or after prayer.
